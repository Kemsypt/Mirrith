Rogue Class Features

Levels 1-7
Level 1 - Sneak Attack

Level 1 - Sneak Attack

Once per turn, deal an extra 4d6 damage to one creature you hit if you have advantage, or if another enemy of the target is within 5 ft of it and you do not have disadvantage. The attack must use a finesse or ranged weapon.

Tips:

  • Aim to trigger this every turn.
  • Use hiding, Faerie Fire, Help, or ally positioning to qualify.
  • Ready actions can sometimes let you Sneak Attack outside your own turn.
Level 1 - Thieves' Cant

Level 1 - Thieves' Cant

You know rogue jargon, coded phrases, symbols, and signs that hide messages inside ordinary conversation or markings.

Tips:

  • Use it to communicate risk without alerting outsiders.
  • Leave signs for allies during infiltration.
  • Ask whether criminal contacts recognise the code.
Level 2 - Cunning Action

Level 2 - Cunning Action

You can take Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a bonus action on each of your turns.

Tips:

  • Your positioning tool every round.
  • Hide to set up advantage and Sneak Attack.
  • Disengage after striking to stay alive.
Level 5 - Uncanny Dodge

Level 5 - Uncanny Dodge

When an attacker you can see hits you, use your reaction to halve that attack's damage.

Tips:

  • Save it for big hits, not tiny chip damage.
  • Competes with Shield and opportunity attacks for your reaction.
  • Best when you know only one heavy hit is coming.
Level 7 - Evasion

Level 7 - Evasion

When a Dexterity save would deal half damage on success, you take no damage on success and only half damage on failure.

Tips:

  • Great against fireballs, breath weapons, and traps.
  • Stay mobile and use cover to force Dex-save situations.
  • Pairs well with high Dexterity and rogue positioning.

Arcane Trickster Features

Roguish Archetype
Level 3 - Spellcasting

Level 3 - Spellcasting

You learn wizard spells, mostly enchantment and illusion. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability. Current slots: 3 first-level.

Tips:

  • Use magic to create advantage, cover, confusion, and openings.
  • Save Shield for emergencies.
  • Control and utility usually support your rogue tactics better than raw blasting.
Level 3 - Mage Hand Legerdemain

Level 3 - Mage Hand Legerdemain

Your Mage Hand becomes invisible and can stow or retrieve objects, manipulate containers, and use thieves' tools at range.

Tips:

  • Use it before entering danger.
  • Open containers, lift keys, and test traps from safety.
  • It is perfect for distractions and subtle theft.

Racial/Background Features

Fairy / Planar Traveler
Fairy Magic

Fairy Magic

You know Druidcraft. You can also use Faerie Fire and Enlarge/Reduce once per long rest, or cast them with spell slots when available.

Tips:

  • Faerie Fire is excellent Sneak Attack setup.
  • Enlarge/Reduce can solve problems creatively outside combat.
  • Druidcraft is a strong roleplay and scouting flavour tool.
Flight

Flight

You have a natural flying speed while not wearing medium or heavy armor.

Tips:

  • Use flight for scouting, angles, and safe positioning.
  • Stay aware of ranged enemies and fall risks.
  • Flight can make hiding and infiltration easier.
Plane Marked

Plane Marked

Elementals can sense planar marks on you and may treat you as touched by many planes, sometimes opening dialogue before hostility.

Tips:

  • Use this as a social opening with planar beings.
  • Mention your marks when negotiating with elementals.
  • Can create story hooks and non-combat solutions.

How to use Familiars

A familiar follows the normal action rules for creatures in D&D 5e, with one major restriction: it cannot take the Attack action. Everything else depends on its stat block and the situation.

Here are the key actions a familiar can perform:

Standard Actions

Dash

Move extra distance for the turn.

Disengage

Move without provoking opportunity attacks.

Dodge

Impose disadvantage on attacks against it and gain advantage on Dex saves until next turn.

Help

Grant advantage to an ally:

  • In combat: give advantage on an attack against a creature within 5 ft of the familiar
  • Outside combat: assist with tasks (DM discretion)

Hide

Attempt to become unseen (Stealth check required).

Ready

Prepare an action to trigger later, such as “Help when the fighter attacks”.

Search

Actively look for hidden creatures or objects using Perception or Investigation.

Use an Object

Interact with items such as opening doors, carrying small objects, or pressing switches, depending on the DM.

Deliver a Touch Spell (Reaction)

When you cast a touch spell, your familiar can deliver it if within 100 ft, using its reaction.

Scout / Perceive

Often involves Search or passive perception while you observe through its senses.

What It Cannot Do

  • Attack — explicitly forbidden by the spell.
  • Take actions its form physically can’t perform, such as opening doors without limbs unless the DM allows it.

Practical Notes

  • Help is the most important combat action. Familiars are often used to grant advantage every round.
  • Disengage + movement = survivability positioning. Especially relevant for flying familiars like owls.
  • Hide + scouting = core utility. Most familiars shine outside combat.

Familiar Type

When you summon a familiar, its spirit can be Celestial, Fey, or Fiend. This changes its creature type without changing the familiar form’s basic stat block.

Celestial

A radiant or divine spirit. Useful for holy, guardian, or otherworldly flavour.

Fey

A whimsical, arcane, or nature-touched spirit. This fits Mirr'ith Alistr'yn’s fey theme especially well.

Fiend

A darker spirit with infernal or abyssal flavour. The familiar still follows the same Find Familiar restrictions.

Sneak Attack

Once per turn, you can deal an extra 4d6 damage to one creature you hit with an attack if you have advantage on the attack roll. The attack must use a finesse or a ranged weapon.

You don't need advantage on the attack roll if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet of it, that enemy isn't incapacitated, and you don't have disadvantage on the attack roll.

Two-Weapon Fighting

This can’t be used with Green-Flame Blade.

When you take the Attack action using a light melee weapon in one hand (such as a dagger, shortsword, scimitar, or handaxe), you can use a bonus action to make an extra attack with a different light melee weapon in your other hand.

You don’t add your ability modifier to the damage of this bonus attack, unless the modifier is negative.

If a weapon has the thrown property (like a dagger or handaxe), you can throw it instead of making a melee attack with it.
